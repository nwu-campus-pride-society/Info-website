The basics of the LGBTQIAP+ community and how to be a good ally!

The L stands for Lesbian.

Lesbain refers to a woman who is attracted to other women.

The G stands for Gay.

A gay person is anyone who is attracted to the same gender.
It has also become an umbrella term for the LGBTQIAP+ community.

The B is for Bisexual and/or Bigender

Bisexual people are usually attracted to two or more genders.
Bigender people are people who generally have two genders.

The T stands for Transgender

Transgender is a general label to describe anyone whose gender is not their Assigned Gender at Birth.

Q is for Queer and/or Questioning

Queer is a general term used to describe anyone who is not cisgender or heterosexual.
Questioning refers to anyone who is questioning their gender/sexual/romantic identity.

The I stands for Intersex

Intersex is an umbrella term for anyone who does not fit in the male/female sex binary.

The A stands for Asexual, Aromantic and/or Agender

Asexuality describes a lack of sexual attraction, and Aromantism describes a lack of romantic attraction.
Similarly, Agender simply describes a lack of gender

The P is for Pansexual and/or Pangender

Pansexual refers to attraction to all genders.
Pangender refers to identifying with all genders

What does the queer community believe in?


Equal and Equitable rights

Everyone and Anyone should and ought to have the same rights.


Intersectionality

Acknowledging that everyone's experience with their identity, and discrimination, is unique.

Rejecting Cis- and Heteronormativity

Acknowledging that cisgender and Heteronormativity should not be the norm/expected identity of everyone.

How can one be a good ally?

Being an ally is not that difficult! Sure, there are some things that might not seem like not being an ally, but nonetheless here are some general guidelines:

  • First thing is first: Never, never assume somebody's gender and/or sexuality
  • Another very simple thing one can do to show that you are a supportive, is to introduce yourself with your name and then your pronuns. For example, "Hi, my name is 'insert name here' and my pronouns are 'insert pronouns here'".
  • On the topic of pronouns, always respect someone else's pronouns! He, She, They, Xe, Xer, Xim, are some examples!
  • Always respect people's chosen names! Whatever they tell you to address them by, use that!
  • Never tell anyone else about someone you know's sexuality and/or gender if the person did not give you their explicit permission.
  • Stand up against discrimination! Call out any dsicrimination and support queer people where and when you can!
  • When standing up against discrimination, be sure you aren't speaking over the queer people that are affected. Be sure to listen and/or talk to them first.
  • Educate yourself on topics you might not be well versed in. It is okay to not know everything! But don't ask people intrusive/personal questions unless they have given you permission to do so
